A big share of roofing revenue runs through insurance, and insurance work lives or dies on details. The claim number. The carrier. When the damage happened. Whether an adjuster has already been out. Miss those on the first call, and you pay for it twice: once when your estimator arrives half-blind, and again when the claim stalls because a piece of information was never collected.

The fix is not complicated. It is capturing the right details up front, every time, without depending on whoever happened to answer the phone. Let us get tactical.

First, a plain note: OneBy captures the details you configure and hands them to your team. It does not give insurance or legal advice, and it does not decide claims. Think of it as a very reliable intake clerk, not an adjuster.

## Why the first call matters more than it looks

By the time a homeowner calls a roofer about storm damage, they are usually already somewhere in the insurance process. Maybe the claim is filed, maybe an adjuster is scheduled, maybe they have no idea where to start. If your intake does not find out which, your estimator walks in blind and has to run the discovery conversation on the driveway. That wastes the visit and slows everything behind it.

A good first call does two things at once. It qualifies the lead, and it front-loads the information your estimator and office need so the next step is ready the moment the call ends.

> An estimator who arrives knowing the carrier, the claim status, and the damage type does a real inspection. One who arrives cold spends the visit asking questions that should have been answered on the phone.

## The details worth capturing every time

You know your own process best, and this list is a starting point, not gospel. But most roofing insurance intake benefits from capturing a consistent core:

- **The basics**: name, property address, best callback number, and how they heard about you.
- **The damage**: what they are seeing (missing shingles, leaks, hail dents, wind damage), which part of the roof, and when they think it happened.
- **The insurance picture**: carrier name, whether a claim has been filed, the claim number if they have it, and whether an adjuster has already inspected or is scheduled.
- **The timing**: how urgent it feels, whether there is active leaking, and when they can be home for an inspection.

Notice what this does. By the time the call ends, the office knows whether this is a fresh claim to help start, a filed claim waiting on an adjuster, or a denied or underpaid claim that might need a supplement. Three very different jobs, sorted at intake instead of at the curb.

### Fresh claim, no adjuster yet

Here the homeowner often needs guidance on the process itself. Your estimator can come prepared to document damage thoroughly, since that documentation may shape the claim. Capturing "no claim filed yet" on the first call tells your team to bring the camera and the patience.

### Claim filed, adjuster scheduled

Now timing is everything. Knowing the adjuster date up front lets your office decide whether to meet the adjuster on site, a move that can matter a great deal for the outcome. That only happens if the first call captured the date.

### Denied, underpaid, or supplement territory

These calls need your most experienced person, not a rushed inspection. Flagging them at intake means they get routed to the right estimator instead of treated like a standard first look.

## The real problem is consistency, not knowledge

Any good roofer knows what to ask. The trouble is that it does not get asked the same way every time. On a quiet Tuesday, your best office person runs a flawless intake. On the Monday after a storm, a crew member grabs the phone between roofs, scribbles a name and number on a sticky note, and the carrier and claim status never get captured at all.

That inconsistency is where claims stall and visits get wasted. The knowledge is in your team's heads. It just does not survive a busy day.
